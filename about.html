2016 – Concept Development

IBM and marine research partners begin exploring the idea of an autonomous, AI-powered ship for ocean research.

2019 – Project Launch

The Mayflower Autonomous Ship (MAS) project officially launches, combining IBM’s AI, edge computing, and automation with advanced marine engineering.

March 2020 – Ship Construction Completed

The vessel is completed in Plymouth, UK, featuring solar power, sensors, and IBM’s edge computing system for autonomous decision-making.

September 2020 – Sea Trials Begin

The MAS begins sea trials in preparation for its first transatlantic mission, testing navigation, obstacle avoidance, and environmental monitoring systems.

June 2021 – First Transatlantic Attempt

The Mayflower departs for its maiden Atlantic voyage. Technical issues force a return, offering valuable data for improvements.

May–June 2022 – Successful Crossing

The MAS completes a successful autonomous journey from the UK to the US (Plymouth to Halifax), marking a major milestone in unmanned marine exploration.

2023–Present – Ongoing Research Missions

The Mayflower continues operating as a floating lab, collecting data on marine life, pollution, and climate change — advancing AI applications in ocean science.
